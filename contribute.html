Contributing to FPiGA Open Source

We welcome contributions to our open-source efforts! Currently, our primary open-source project is the FPiGA Core Library, and we encourage community involvement to help shape its future.

To contribute, we recommend the following steps:

1. Report Issues or Propose Features
If you encounter a bug or have an idea for a new feature, please open an issue or start a discussion on the FPiGA Core Library GitHub repository. Be sure to describe the issue or proposal in detail, along with your intended approach for addressing it.

2. Collaboration and Development
Once your idea is reviewed, we’ll work with you to define a clear path toward integrating your contribution into the development branch. Contributions will undergo testing and refinement in the dev branch until they're considered stable enough for a mainline release.

At this stage, the FPiGA project is maintained solely by the Radical Computer Technologies (RCT) team. However, as community engagement grows, we are open to launching a dedicated Community Edition — a fork managed by a team of active open-source contributors focused on maintaining and evolving the project collaboratively.

Your participation can directly shape the future of FPiGA — we look forward to building it with you.
